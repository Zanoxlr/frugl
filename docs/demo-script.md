# SubSmart — Hackathon Demo Script & Pitch

B2C multi-utility subscription advisor. Slovenian market. POC.

---

## 1. Positioning

### One-liner
Not a bill-lowering service. A pre-sales needs-clarifier that turns a passive, upsellable consumer into an informed buyer, then hands providers a lead that already knows what it wants.

### The problem in one breath
Every incumbent works the same way: "send us your bill, we'll lower it." The consumer stays passive and uninformed. So when a human sales agent finally calls, the consumer can't push back, and gets upsold on the exact stuff they came to cut. The bill "comparison" is bait; the call is the sell.

SubSmart flips the order. Inform first, qualify second, hand off last. An informed consumer can't be upsold, and a lead that already understands its own needs is worth more, not less, to the provider.

### Name candidates
- **SubSmart** (current working title) — clear, does-what-it-says, slightly generic.
- **Jasno** — Slovenian for "clear / obvious." One word, local, and the whole pitch is "now it's clear what you actually pay for." Strong.
- **Vestnik** — "the informed one / conscientious." Warmer, trust-first framing. Riskier (reads a bit formal).

Recommendation: keep **SubSmart** for the demo (English-friendly for judges), flag **Jasno** as the market name for a Slovenian consumer launch.

### 30-second elevator pitch
"Slovenians overpay on utilities not because they picked wrong, but because nobody ever showed them what they actually have. The 'send us your bill' services keep them in the dark on purpose, because a confused customer is an easy upsell. SubSmart puts all your subscriptions, telco, energy, insurance, water, in one dashboard, then a grounded AI advisor walks you through what you're really paying for and what you can drop. It catches the stuff a salesperson would never mention: a legacy health charge you no longer owe, a sports package you never watch. Once you know your real needs, we match you to a right-fit offer, and we sell that high-intent lead to the provider. They pay for fit, not volume. Informed buyer, cleaner lead, everyone but the upseller wins."

## 2. The demo persona — Marko

**Marko, 34, Ljubljana.** Software tester, lives with his partner in a 2-room flat in Šiška. Busy, mildly annoyed by admin, hasn't reviewed a single subscription in three years. Pays everything by direct debit and never looks. Typical.

His five subscriptions, as SubSmart sees them after onboarding:

| Vertical | Provider (demo) | What Marko pays | The rigged gotcha |
|---|---|---|---|
| Telco (mobile + TV/net) | Telco operator, family package | ~45 EUR/mo incl. **Arena Sport add-on ~8 EUR/mo** | He added Arena during a football season two years ago. Hasn't opened it in months. Pure dead weight. |
| Energy (elektrika + plin) | Standard tariff | ~120 EUR/mo | On the **standard/default tariff** since caps ended March 2025. A current promo tariff at a lower per-kWh rate would cut it. He never re-checked because "the price cap had it handled" — but caps are gone. |
| Health insurance | Legacy insurer charge | **~35 EUR/mo "dopolnilno"** | This is the big one. Dopolnilno zdravstveno was **abolished in 2024** and replaced by the compulsory state charge **OZP (~37 EUR/mo)**. Marko pays OZP through payroll AND is still paying his old insurer a redundant legacy health charge on top. Double-paying. |
| Car / other insurance | Motor policy | ~40 EUR/mo | Carries **GAP** and **replacement-car / luggage riders** on a car he **owns outright** (paid off, no lease/loan). GAP is pointless once the car isn't financed. Over-bought. |
| Water | Municipal utility | ~25 EUR/mo | Nothing to sell. Municipal monopoly, no switching. The advisor just explains the bill honestly. This is the trust anchor. |

**Total on the dashboard: ~265 EUR/mo.**
**What an honest advisor can strip or fix without downgrading his life: Arena (~8), redundant health charge (~35), GAP/riders (part of ~40), plus the energy re-shop.** Real, defensible savings north of ~50 EUR/mo before we even touch the energy switch.

The point of the rig: two of the gotchas are things a normal comparison site would never catch (the OZP double-pay, the GAP-on-an-owned-car), and one (water) is where we deliberately *don't* sell. That combination is the whole story.

## 3. Stage flow — tap by tap (~2–3 min)

Keep narration tight. The dashboard and the anti-upsell reveal are the two shots that land; everything else is glue.

**[0:00] Onboarding (10s).**
Open app. "Marko just linked his accounts." One tap through a consent screen ("SubSmart reads your bills, never changes anything"). Loading shimmer, then the dashboard populates. Do NOT dwell here.

> Say: "No forms. He connects, we read. Notice we never asked him to upload a bill and wait for a human to call back."

**[0:15] Dashboard — 5 tiles + total (20s).**
Five clean tiles: Telco, Energija, Zdravstveno, Zavarovanje, Voda. Big total at top: **265 EUR / mesec.** Each tile shows the monthly number and a small status dot (green = looks fine, amber = worth a look). Three amber dots: Telco, Energija, Zdravstveno.

> Say: "Everything he pays for, one screen. He's never seen this before. Three things are already flagged. Let's open the telco one."

**[0:35] Tap Telco → the grounded chat moment (35s).**
Tile expands into a chat with the advisor. The advisor opens with what it actually knows, not a sales line:

> Advisor: "Na tem paketu imaš mobilni, internet in TV, plus dodatek Arena Sport za 8 EUR na mesec. Arena je ločen dodatek, ni vključen v osnovni paket. Zadnjih 90 dni je nisi odprl. Ti jo pustimo ali odstranimo?"

This is the grounded beat. Now ask it the rehearsed factual questions live (section 4) so the judges see it answer accurately about the real package, not vibe.

> Say: "This is the difference. It's not guessing, it's not selling. It knows exactly what's on his plan and it's telling him the add-on is dead weight. A sales agent's job is to keep that Arena on the bill."

**[1:10] 4-question needs discovery (30s).**
Advisor runs a short, plain needs check. Four questions, tap-to-answer chips:

1. "Koliko naprav imamo doma na netu?" → 5–7
2. "Gledaš šport v živo?" → "skoraj nikoli"
3. "Rabiš TV pakete ali večinoma streamaš?" → "večinoma Netflix/YouTube"
4. "Je avto tvoj ali na leasing/kredit?" → "moj, odplačan"

The last question looks like it belongs to insurance, not telco. That's intentional: it sets up the cross-vertical reveal.

> Say: "Four questions. Notice the last one is about his car. The advisor is building his real profile across everything, not just this one bill."

**[1:40] The needs card + the anti-upsell reveal (30s).**
Advisor produces a **Needs Card**. The dramatic bit: it visibly strips features Marko said he doesn't need. On screen, line items animate out with a strike-through:

> **Tvoj profil**
> ~~Arena Sport (8 EUR)~~ — ne gledaš športa v živo → **odstrani**
> ~~GAP kritje na avtu~~ — avto je odplačan, GAP je smiseln samo pri leasingu/kreditu → **ne rabiš**
> ~~Legacy dopolnilno (35 EUR)~~ — dopolnilno je 2024 ukinjeno, OZP že plačuješ → **podvojeno plačilo, ukini**
> ✓ Internet za 5–7 naprav — **obdrži**
> ✓ Voda — občinski monopol, nič za menjat, tvoj račun je korekten

The three struck-through lines are the money shot. The advisor is *removing* things from what it will sell him.

> Say (slow down here): "Watch what it just did. It took things OFF. No sports package he doesn't watch. No GAP cover on a car he already owns. And this one — he's been paying his insurer 35 euros a month for dopolnilno health cover that was abolished in 2024. He already pays the state OZP charge for the same thing through his salary. He's been double-paying for two years. No comparison site catches that. And notice: on water, we tell him to do nothing. We don't sell when there's nothing to sell. That's why he trusts the rest."

**[2:10] Book the right offer → lead saved (20s).**
One clean recommendation card: a right-fit energy tariff (lower per-kWh, matched to his actual usage) and a slimmed telco/insurance profile. Button: **"Poveži me s ponudnikom."** Tap. Confirmation: "Tvoj profil je poslan. Ponudnik ve točno kaj rabiš."

> Say: "One button. The lead that goes to the provider isn't 'guy who wants a cheaper bill.' It's 'Marko, 5–7 devices, no live sports, owns his car, needs energy re-priced to his real usage, don't bother pitching him TV sports or GAP.' That lead converts, and it doesn't churn, because he already knows it fits. That's what a provider pays us for."

**Total run time: ~2:10.** If time is tight, cut the 4-question section to two questions and keep the reveal full-length. Never cut the reveal.

## 4. Rehearsed factual questions to ask live

Ask 3 of these during the telco chat beat. They prove the advisor is grounded on the real package, not improvising. Pick based on time.

1. **"Je Arena na tem paketu zastonj?"**
   Expected: "Ne. Arena Sport je ločen dodatek za 8 EUR na mesec, ni vključen v osnovni paket. Zadnjih 90 dni je nisi uporabil." (Grounded: knows it's a paid add-on AND that it's unused.)

2. **"Kolko kanalov dobim na tem paketu?"**
   Expected: a specific channel count for his actual tier, then: "Če večinoma streamaš, plačuješ za pakete ki jih ne gledaš." (Grounded on his tier, then honest.)

3. **"Zakaj mi je zdravstveno označeno rdeče?"**
   Expected: "Ker plačuješ staro dopolnilno zavarovanje pri zavarovalnici. To je bilo 2024 ukinjeno in nadomeščeno z obveznim prispevkom OZP (~37 EUR), ki ga že plačuješ. Torej plačuješ dvakrat za isto stvar." (The wow fact, delivered on demand.)

4. **"A mi splača menjat ponudnika elektrike?"**
   Expected: "Od marca 2025 ni več cenovne kape, cene so tržne. Tvoj strošek = poraba × cena na kWh + fiksni del. Na standardni tarifi si; obstaja promo tarifa z nižjo ceno na kWh za tvojo porabo. Ja, splača se pogledat." (Grounded on the post-cap reality and the real cost formula, not a flat "switch and save.")

**Safety line if asked something out of data:** the advisor should say "Tega podatka nimam, ne bom ugibal" rather than invent. Have one such question ready if a judge tries to break it.

## 5. Judge Q&A prep

**(a) "You promise not to upsell, but you sell leads to sellers. Why does that work / isn't that the same thing?"**
Different transaction. Incumbents sell *volume* — any warm body, upsold as hard as possible, high churn. We sell *fit*. The provider pays for a pre-qualified, high-intent lead that already understands its own needs. Those leads convert at a much higher rate and churn far less, because the customer bought something that actually fits instead of being talked into extras. The provider's economics reward fit over volume: lower CAC-to-retention, fewer cancellations, less support load. We're not hiding the sale, we're removing the manipulation from it. The consumer gets an honest match; the provider gets a customer that sticks. The only loser is the upsell.

**(b) "4 of your 5 verticals are fake / mocked, right?"**
No, all five run on real data now. Energy is grounded on the ZPS (Zveza potrošnikov Slovenije) energy pricing survey plus live-scraped operator tariff pages, so the per-kWh comparison is real post-cap math. Telco and insurance pull from live-scraped operator and insurer pages for package contents and add-on pricing. Health is grounded on the actual 2024 dopolnilno→OZP regulatory change. Water is real municipal tariff data. The demo persona is scripted, the facts behind every answer are not.

**(c) "What stops the AI from hallucinating?"**
It's grounded-only. Every answer is tied to a known data point about the user's actual plan or a verified regulatory fact. When the data isn't there, it refuses: "Tega podatka nimam, ne bom ugibal." It doesn't fill gaps with plausible-sounding numbers. You can try to break it live — ask it something it can't know and watch it decline instead of invent. That refusal behavior is the feature, because a single wrong number destroys the trust the whole model depends on.

**(d) "Isn't this just a comparison site with a chatbot on top?"**
No. A comparison site answers "who's cheapest" for a product you already decided you want. It keeps you passive. The whitespace here is *conversational needs-definition plus anti-upsell*: we work out what you actually need before anyone shows you an offer, and we visibly remove things you don't need, including things we could have sold you. A comparison site would never tell you to drop your GAP cover or that your health charge is redundant — there's no affiliate payout in telling you to buy less. The output isn't a price ranking, it's a pre-sales needs profile. That profile is the product, for the consumer and for the provider. Nobody else builds that.

## Appendix — fact sheet (verified July 2026)

- **Dopolnilno zdravstveno abolished 2024**, replaced by compulsory state charge **OZP ~37 EUR/mo**. Many still pay a legacy/redundant health charge to an insurer on top. Advisor catches the double-pay.
- **Energy price caps ended March 2025** → prices market-set, real comparison possible. Cost = usage (kWh) × per-kWh price + fixed fee. Not a flat bill.
- **Insurance riders — who actually needs what:** GAP only matters for leased/financed cars; drop it on an owned car. Luggage / replacement-car cover is usually over-buying.
- **Telco add-ons:** paid extras like Arena Sport (~8 EUR/mo) that people pay for and don't use.
- **Water = municipal monopoly, no switching.** Advisor only explains the bill honestly. This is a deliberate no-sell, and it's what earns trust for everything else.
